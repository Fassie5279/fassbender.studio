Words

Sometimes I like to write simple poems. Looking back at them, I've noticed that a recurring theme always seems to emerge: the concept of time and how it passes. It isn’t intentional; it’s just a theme that 'weaves' its way into my writing unconsciously.

I have collected these poems below, having edited them slightly for this page, and I've also included the scans of the original, typewritten first drafts at the end.

'Us' is a short poem honoring love and beautiful memories. 

Us

When we're old, let's think of us like when, in text messages small and grey, the world was beautiful for a day.

When discoveries were made, and uncertainties were framed on Nokias, in simple wordplay.

When bursts of light in all directions were shot away. Forever to stay. In the cosmos where our hearts, will never give way.

'The Best Years' is an attempt to capture fading moments. I think it's a plea against forgetting. Perhaps a realisation that nothing lasts forever. 

 

The Best Years

The best years come like a sigh of wind with blossom leaves that blanket my street. Like an October table with candlelight that warms my soul.

Like the look of the good, which looks at me— in love. With the bridge and the city on the other side, where lights glow.

On the photo of everything that must stay. Like the nights of Osaka, or the attic.

The room of my heart that races like a bullet train: definite, on time. All the time knowing.

The best years— they went unnoticed.

'Grounded' again deals with the concept of time, but this time from a non-human perspective. The poem is an ode to a football stadium—specifically 'De Kuip,' the historic home of Feyenoord in Rotterdam, with its iconic floodlights. 

Built in 1937, the stadium holds a massive history; it was even used by the Nazis during World War II as a assembly point for forced labor deportations. Through the decades since, it has held the collective emotions of the millions who have passed through its gates. Serving almost as a metaphor for life itself, De Kuip has witnessed immense joy, pain, and everything in between. Yet, it still stands—its floodlights continuing to illuminate both the grass and the hearts of many.

 

Grounded

As the clouds passed, there you sat, firm and brave on the land that belonged to you. Like the skies, and the heights, from which you had seen it all. And all looked up to you, from the ground that held no secrets.

The ground where you ate and you fed those who came for you. The water from which you sprung, meandering through the soil, lifting you up on an open hand, where a ladybird rests.

Where good fortune and mishap are all, and all things are, as shaped by the mind that meets them.

Lifelike. Joyful. Battered. Bandaged and saved.

For your story to be told and sprayed on fences and walls—oh, how they divide us all! When we fly off in search of an illusion: a meal, a dream, or a place for the night.

Darkness illuminated by your ever-shining light.
